# Working outline

## Opening idea

AI can amplify judgment, speed, and exploration when it is used to widen the set of options, expose trade-offs, and make thinking more explicit.

## The risk

Used passively, AI can reinforce assumptions instead of testing them. It can become a confident echo chamber: fluent, agreeable, and fast enough to make weak ideas feel stronger than they are.

## Practical angle

The useful pattern is to treat AI as a critical pair programmer, not a validation machine. Ask it to challenge the design, identify failure modes, compare simpler alternatives, and explain what evidence would change the decision.

## Closing placeholder

Practices that keep human judgment in the loop: write down assumptions, ask for risks, test against real constraints, prefer small reversible steps, and keep responsibility with the engineer.
