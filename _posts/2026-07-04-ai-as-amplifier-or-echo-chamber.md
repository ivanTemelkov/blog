# AI as an Amplifier vs. AI as an Echo Chamber

AI is not a magic solution for every problem.

It is better understood as an **amplifier** of our potential.

I often see people say that with AI, a good engineer will become better, while a bad engineer will become worse. I can see the logic in that statement. AI can definitely multiply both good and bad habits.

But I do not think the idea is complete.

There is another risk: the **echo chamber**.

We already saw this with the rise of social media platforms. People started receiving more and more content that confirmed what they already believed. Different points of view became easier to avoid. Opinions became stronger, but not always better informed.

With AI, this risk may become even greater.

An AI assistant can help us write, organize ideas, and move faster. But it can also make a weak idea sound convincing. It can agree too easily. It can polish our assumptions instead of challenging them.

At that point, AI is not helping us think better.

It is only helping us hear our own thoughts more clearly.

## So, what can we do?

I think the answer is simple, but not easy.

We should **stay involved**.

AI can suggest, draft, explain, and accelerate. But it should not replace our judgment.

We need to **keep asking questions**:

- Does this really make sense?
- What am I missing?
- Is there another point of view?
- Am I accepting this because it is correct, or because it sounds good?

For me, the best use of AI is not to avoid thinking.

It is to **think better**.
